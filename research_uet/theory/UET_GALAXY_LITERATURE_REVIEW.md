## 1. The Core Problem: Missing Mass vs. Modified Gravity
The observed flatness of galaxy rotation curves remains one of the greatest unsolved problems in physics.
*   **Newtonian Expectation:** $v \propto 1/\sqrt{r}$. Velocity should drop at the edge.
*   **Observation:** $v \approx \text{const}$. Velocity stays flat.

*   **Hypothesis:** Halo of invisible "Dark Matter" particles (WIMPs).
*   **Problem:** The "Cusp-Core" problem. Simulations predict high-density centers (cusps), but observations show constant-density centers (cores).
*   **Density Dependence:** Standard Dark Matter models often fail to explain why the dark matter distribution seems to track the baryonic (visible) matter so closely (the "Renzo's Rule").

### 1.2 Modified Newtonian Dynamics (MOND)
*   **Hypothesis:** Gravity is modified at low accelerations ($a < a_0 \approx 1.2 \times 10^{-10} m/s^2$).
*   **Success:** Perfectly predicts the "Radial Acceleration Relation" (RAR) observed in the SPARC database.
*   **Limitation:** It is an *empirical fit*. It describes *how* gravity changes, but not *why*.

### 1.3 Vacuum Modified Gravity (Emerging Field)
